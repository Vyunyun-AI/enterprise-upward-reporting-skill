# Enterprise Upward Reporting Skill

A reusable agentic skill package for optimizing upward reporting drafts in line with Chinese mainland SOE governance, compliance, and formal official writing requirements.

## Overview

This project provides a structured skill package for transforming draft reports into formal upward-reporting documents suitable for Chinese mainland state-owned enterprise governance contexts. It is designed to strengthen reporting quality across three core dimensions:

- political alignment
- business and KPI grounding
- compliance and evidence-chain closure

Rather than simply polishing language, this skill restructures content, upgrades logic, and aligns expression with formal official writing norms commonly used in modern SOE governance and regulatory reporting environments.

## What This Skill Does

This skill helps convert rough or semi-structured drafts into more formal and policy-aligned reporting texts by:

- restructuring the draft into standardized reporting formats
- improving political framing and governance alignment
- strengthening KPI interpretation and business logic
- reinforcing compliance, accountability, and remediation closure
- replacing informal language with disciplined official-writing rhetoric

## Applicable Scenarios

This skill is mainly designed for:

- status and progress reports
- issue explanation and official reply materials
- inspection or audit rectification reports
- internal governance reporting drafts
- materials requiring stronger formal tone, clearer logic, and tighter compliance framing

## Core Design Principles

The skill is built around three integrated principles:

### 1. Political Positioning
The report should clearly connect operational matters to broader policy direction, governance expectations, and institutional responsibility.

### 2. Business Data Grounding
The report should not stop at listing figures. It should explain KPI movement through underlying business logic and operational context.

### 3. Compliance Closure
The report should move from abstract statements to evidence-supported accountability, with clear corrective actions, owners, timelines, and control logic.
